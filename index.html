Native Americans
By: Shreya

Desert Southwest People

The Desert Southwest People also called the Pueblo People are a group of Native Americans who lived in the 4 corner states which are Colorado, Utah, New Mexico and Arizona.

Tribes: 2 of the tribes among this region are Hopi and the Zuni. The Navajo tribe were nomads which means they didn't have a permanent home. This tribe later settled in the Desert Southwest Region and had a similar culture to the Hopi.

Food: The Pueblo People grew corn, beans, and squash. Even in the dry, hot climate, the Pueblo people used something called Irrigation. We still use it today! Irrigation is the use of pipes to move water. The Pueblo People also grew cotton which they used to make blankets and clothing.

Shelter: The Navojo Tribe used to build houses called Hogans. They had a wooden frame and were covered in Adobe Bricks.

Pacific Northwest People

The Pacific Northwest People lived in Present-Day Oregon, Washington and Western Canada.

Tribes: Some tribes that lived in the Pacific Northwest Region or the Northwest Coast Region are named the Kwakiutl, Makaw, Chinook.

Food: These tribes hunted whales. They ate the whales and used the fat for burning lamps. They also fished and gathered plants and nuts.

Shelter: The Pacific Northwest People built longhouses which are rectangular homes that can go up to 60 feet long.

Eastern Woodlands

The Eastern Woodland People lived East of the Missisipi River.

Tribes: The Iroquis lived near the Great Lake Area.

Food: They grew Corn, Beans and Squash which they called the 3 sisters because they helped each other grow.

Shelter: The Eastern Woodland people built their villages along rivers or streams. They had palisades or wooden pole fences around their villages.

Plain People

The Plain People lived in the Inteior Plains which is between the Missisipi River and the Rocky Mountains. Today, the Interior Plains are divided into 2 parts, the Central Plains and the Great Plains.

Tribes: Some Tribes were Mandan, Pawnee, Wichita and the Sioux.

Food: They mainly depended on buffalo for food and clothing but they also farmed.

Shelter: They Plain People built houses called Lodges which were circular shaped homes.

Arctic People

The Arctic People lived near the north pole.

Tribes: Some tribes were the Aleut named after the Aleautioan Islands and the Inuit Tribe.

Food: They used kayaks to hunt and fish. They hunted whales for oil, food and clothing. They also hunted arctic foxes and polar bears.

Shelter: The Aluet and the Inuit built igloos which were made of ice for the winter and made tents for the summer.
